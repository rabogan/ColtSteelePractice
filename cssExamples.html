Using common text properties

  • In the h1, we're aligning the text to the center
  • In the h1, we're also changing the font weight (boldness/lightness): 400 being normal, 700 being bold. H1 is usually bold, but is now set to 400
  • Text decoration, or text-decoration is a way of setting the appearance of decorative lines on text! I've set h1 to be underlined in red with a wavy line!
  • How to remove all underlines from web links? Well, just set text-decoration of the anchor (a) to none!
  • To double-space your paragraphs (as below), set the line-height to 2!
  • Letter-spacing adds some distance between letters! For this example, I added 15 pixels of letter-spacing in h1

We can look at different font families on www.cssfontstack.com Why font stack? Well, generally, we don't always know if our font will work across all machines (Windows, Mac, Chrome, whatever). So, in our font-family part of our specific element, we list a stack of fonts - in order from most preferred and specific, to most generic. font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif

At a later point, we will include custom fonts, and guarantee that the user will be able to access them (possibly at the expense of loading time)
